Mitosis

In cell biology, mitosis (/maɪˈtoʊsɪs/) is a part of the cell cycle in which replicated

chromosomes are separated into two new nuclei. Cell division by mitosis gives rise to

genetically identical cells in which the total number of chromosomes is maintained.
